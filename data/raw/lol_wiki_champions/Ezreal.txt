On-attack
effects refer to various instances that are triggered by the user when they finish their basic attack windup.
Unlike on-hit effects, on-attack effects do not require the user to land their basic attacks, only to finish their windup. Abilities that trigger on-attack effects will do so upon successfully hitting the target.

A

, and the

Turrets

Inhibitors

The

Basic attack
The
basic attack
(or "auto") is the default means by which a unit deals damage, usually equaling
100% AD
physical damage
.
Basic attacks
are normally unit-targeted
edge range
spells, and the
attack range
stat determines their reach.
The caster must stand still to complete a brief
windup
before launching a
basic attack
. Depending on the caster:
If a caster's attacks are non-projectile (e.g.
melee
) launching the attack deals damage immediately.
If a caster's attacks are
projectile
, launching the attack sends a projectile that follows the target and deals damage upon striking them.
The caster then incurs a brief cooldown, based on their
attack speed
. Basic attacks' damage may trigger
on-hit effects
only upon dealing damage.
On-attack
effects will always trigger upon the attack's
windup
completing.

Champion ability
A
champion ability
(often just

) is a unique action performed by and only available to a specific
champion
. All of these special moves available to a champion collectively constitute their

. A skill point is earned each time the champion

Champion abilities

and

Active effects are

resource
, such as
mana
.
Passive effects may simply be permanently available or are
cast
by fulfilling a requirement or using a specific command.
Most abilities incur a
cooldown
after being cast, during which they cannot be used. This cooldown time is usually reduced by
ability haste
.
Spell effects
may trigger only when a valid ability deals damage. On-cast effects trigger upon an ability's
cast time
completing.
