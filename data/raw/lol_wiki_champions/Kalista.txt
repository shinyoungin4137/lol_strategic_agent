Basic attacks and
Pierce
cannot be cast while dashing, unless animation cancelled with a movement command.
Kalista
must wait for her auto attack windup or cast animation to finish, before she dashes. However,
Kalista
will dash immediately if the movement command to dash is input after her auto attack windup finishes.
Martial Poise
also "remembers" your auto attack target after dashing, letting you dash repeatedly by only clicking the ground.
Martial Poise
cannot keep up with attack speed values greater than 2.
1
, due to the combination of her auto attack windup taking too long, and her dashes being too slow. Movement speed bonuses make it less of an issue.
Sufficiently high attack speeds can also cause
Martial Poise
to interpret movement inputs as dash inputs, during the dash of a previous auto attack (such as jumping towards attack-move inputs).
Move commands inputted during an attack windup or the cast time of
Pierce
while
Kalista
is
immobilized
or
grounded
will fail to cast and thus prevent the dash from triggering.
If multiple move commands are inputted during the attack windup, the most recent one is used for the dash's targeting.
If the most recent input is not a move command, the dash will not trigger.
In this case, the buffer for the previous move command was cancelled by a new non-move command such as an input for an attack or ability cast.
The move command will still be buffered to the end of the immobilization or the end of the attack windup or cast time of
Pierce
while grounded if it has not been overridden by other inputs.
Kalista's
facing direction will change immediately on move command even among multiple inputs, but will not visually update until the dash.
The facing direction when dashing is considered to be in the direction of the dash, not her apparent facing direction based on her model's animation state.
Dash distance decreases as the dash direction becomes closer to the minimum direction. This defines a forward distance for basic attacks and a backwards distance for
Pierce
.

Attack speed ratio
The
Attack speed ratio (AS ratio)
adjusts the effectiveness of bonus AS from all sources. It is usually equal to the unit's base AS (level 1 with no bonuses), but tuning to a higher or lower relative value translates to more or less bonus AS received compared to other units with the same base AS.
Total AS = Base AS + Bonus AS ×
AS Ratio
(All values in decimal form)
The ingame stats HUD displays the
AS ratio
instead of base AS to communicate this comparative effectiveness. The Wiki opts to always list both stats and show the
AS ratio
as "N/A" if the two are equal.

Basic attack
The
basic attack
(or "auto") is the default means by which a unit deals damage, usually equaling
100% AD
physical damage
.
Basic attacks
are normally unit-targeted
edge range
spells, and the
attack range
stat determines their reach.
The caster must stand still to complete a brief
windup
before launching a
basic attack
. Depending on the caster:
If a caster's attacks are non-projectile (e.g.
melee
) launching the attack deals damage immediately.
If a caster's attacks are
projectile
, launching the attack sends a projectile that follows the target and deals damage upon striking them.
The caster then incurs a brief cooldown, based on their
attack speed
. Basic attacks' damage may trigger
on-hit effects
only upon dealing damage.
On-attack
effects will always trigger upon the attack's
windup
completing.

Uncancellable windup
Some effects cause a basic attack to have an
uncancellable windup
. Unlike normal basic attacks and similar to the cast time of a spell, the windup of these attacks cannot be
interrupted
by neither the user themselves nor any status effect, other than
death
,
polymorph
and
Quinn's
Vault
, and sometimes

and range.
Any effect that would disrupt an attack's windup, aside from the ones noted above, are ignored by basic attacks with
uncancellable windups
. If the attack doesn't lock out other abilities, they may be used normally, and the basic attack will hit during the ability cast.
